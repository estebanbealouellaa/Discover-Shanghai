About Shanghai

Shanghai, China's largest city, is a global financial hub and cultural melting pot. Known for its modern skyline, the Huangpu River, and historic architecture, Shanghai blends tradition and innovation seamlessly.

With a population of over 24 million, Shanghai is one of the world's most populous cities and a major center for finance, technology, fashion, and culture. The city's iconic landmarks include The Bund, Shanghai Tower, Yu Garden, and the historic French Concession.

Top Places to Visit

The Bund

Walk along the Huangpu River and enjoy breathtaking views of Shanghai's skyline.

Shanghai Tower

The tallest building in China, offering panoramic city views from the observation deck.

Yu Garden

A beautiful classical garden that reflects traditional Chinese art and architecture.

Huangpu River Cruise

The Huangpu River Cruise is a boat tour in Shanghai that lets you see the city's skyline and landmarks along the river.

Shanghai Museum

The Shanghai Museum is a museum in Shanghai that showcases ancient Chinese art and cultural treasures.

Shanghai Disneyland Park

Experience the magic at Shanghai Disneyland, featuring unique attractions and entertainment with Chinese cultural elements.

Taste of Shanghai

Shanghai cuisine, known as Hu cuisine, offers bold yet balanced flavors. Don't miss dishes like Xiaolongbao (soup dumplings), Shengjianbao (pan-fried buns), and Sweet Braised Pork Belly.

Other must-try dishes include Shanghai hairy crab in autumn, drunken chicken, and red-braised pork. The cuisine is characterized by its use of soy sauce, sugar, and rice wine.
